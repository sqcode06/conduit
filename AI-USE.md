# AI use policy

CONDUIT is built with AI assistance. This document sets the boundary between what
AI may touch and what a person must own.

## Code may be AI-assisted

AI tools (e.g. Claude Code) may help write, refactor, and review **code**, tests,
and configuration. Everything AI touches is read and understood by a person before
it lands. Whoever merges it owns it.

## Words are owned by humans

All human-facing prose is authored or owned by a person:

- commit messages
- pull request titles and descriptions
- issue reports and comments
- release notes and `CHANGELOG.md` entries
- security advisories and disclosure correspondence

AI may draft a suggestion, but a person reviews, edits, and owns the published
result. Standard boilerplate (license text, the Contributor Covenant) is the
obvious exception.

## Attribution

The author of a commit message is the human who wrote it. Commits are not
automatically tagged with an AI co-author trailer; if you choose to credit AI
assistance, that's your call to make explicitly.
