# AI use policy

CONDUIT is built with AI assistance, openly. This document draws the line.

## Code may be AI-assisted

AI tools (e.g. Claude Code) may help write, refactor, and review **code**, tests,
and configuration. Everything AI touches is read and understood by a human before
it lands — the human merging it owns it.

## Words are owned by humans

All human-facing prose is authored — or reviewed, edited, and owned — by a person:

- commit messages
- pull request titles and descriptions
- issue reports and comments
- release notes and `CHANGELOG.md` entries
- security advisories and disclosure correspondence

AI may draft a suggestion, but a human reviews it, edits it, and takes ownership —
the published words are a person's, not an autogenerated artifact. Standard
boilerplate (license text, the Contributor Covenant) is the obvious exception.

## Attribution

The author of a commit message is the human who wrote it. Commits are not
automatically tagged with an AI co-author trailer; if you choose to credit AI
assistance, that's your call to make explicitly.
